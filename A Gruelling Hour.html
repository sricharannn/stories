A GRUELLING HOUR

This happened to me in my second branch

Charan works in a Public Sector Bank in Hyderabad. His branch is one of the four biggest branches in India as per volume of the business. Charan is the last in hierarchy in a branch which is headed by a scale VI manager (there are VII scales in a Public Sector Bank). His branch starts at 10:30 A.M. and ends when the manager says so. According to the rule book the junior is not to handle the strong room keys, but usually in Public Sector the junior ends up handling them. In this case Charan handles the keys in that branch.

It started like any regular day. He decided to reach the branch a little bit earlier that day to complete a pending work assigned to him. By the time Charan arrived at the branch at 9:10A.M., which is 20 minutes earlier than his regular time, there already is a senior officer waiting at the main door. He wished the senior officer good morning and proceeded to open the main door. As he didn’t want to be disturbed in the middle of the pending `work, Charan decided to handover the keys to the senior officer

Charan opened the front zip of his bag where he usually places them and casually groped in the bag. But his hands couldn’t get hold of the keys’ pouch in the bag where he usually places it. He frantically searched the whole bag to no avail. Beads of sweat started forming on his brows and above his lips. He thought to himself very hard as to what could have happened to the keys. Just a week back Charan came by the knowledge of what happens to an officer if he lost the keys – Rs.94,000/- fine, two increments cut, charge sheet for the negligence and no promotions for God knows how long.

9:15A.M

Charan started tracing his steps from yesterday night very carefully. His next stop after the Bank was KFC. He treated himself to a nice little Zinger burger meal and went back to his single room which is located some 4kms odd from his branch. He was sure that in all the time he was in KFC he never let the bag from his shoulder. In the room there is no chance of misplacing the pouch. At that point of time Charan wished that he might have opened the bag for something and the keys might have fallen out of the bag. Even during the thought process, he knew that did not happen.

9:20A.M

Charan gave the senior manager some reason to meet his brother for a quick breakfast and promised him that he would return by 10:15A.M. He left his bag and sprinted out of the branch, started his bike and raced towards his room. Even as he raced towards his room, he knew that he wouldn’t find the keys in his room. On the way Charan stopped at the KFC, but the store is still closed. It took him 15 minutes in the traffic to reach his room. Praying to God he searched the whole room, but to no avail. Suddenly Charan felt positive that if he didn’t place the keys in his bag then they should be in his draw and cursed himself for not searching his draw before racing out of the branch. He told himself that everything is going to be okay and that he would find the keys in the draw.

9:45A.M

With this new found positive thought Charan started his bike and raced towards his branch. By the time he reached the branch, all except the branch manager and the other officer who needs to be present to open the safe, are present in the branch. He breathed a sigh of relief and searched for the keys in his draw. He was positive that the keys would be lying in the draw and vowed to be careful next time. But he did not find the keys even in the draw. He knew, his chances of locating the keys are bleak now. But Charan didn’t lose his cool as he still has the KFC to search.

10:05A.M

He immediately grabbed his bag and hopped on his bike and revved towards KFC. There he came to know that the store opens only at 11:00 A.M. But Charan’s branch starts at 10:30 A.M. in just twenty more minutes. Charan lost all the hope. He doesn’t even know for sure if the keys are in KFC or not. What would he answer the Branch Manager? He doesn’t even know where he lost the keys. Charan cursed himself for being so careless. His future seemed completely lost. He just stood there in front of KFC with tears swelling up in his eyes. He decided to go to room and plan his next course of action. Run away to home or commit SUICIDE!!!

10:10A.M

Charan didn’t even know how he reached his room in the heavy traffic. It is 10:20 A.M now and he is not anywhere near locating the keys. With tears in his eyes and hope in his heart he once again opened his bag and searched for one last time. NOTHING. He can think of nothing. He knows that running away to home means end of life as he knows and be completely dependent on his parents. May be a police case for the loss of keys. These thoughts are not at all helping Charan and in frustration he threw the bag against the wall. Something black fell out of the bag. He cleared his tears and to his utter astonishment the keys pouch lay there in front of his eyes.

10:25A.M

He literally flew towards the branch on his bike and entered the branch just in the nick of time before the Branch manager.

Charan didn’t get any time to rejoice or even think about how happy or lucky he was that day. But even today the thought of that ONE HOUR gives him the creeps and chills.
